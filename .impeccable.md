## Design Context

### Users
Herd & Seek is for casual and social players, especially younger players already comfortable with Roblox-style games, playing short sessions or exploring together on desktop, tablet, and phone. They need to understand a mode immediately, join friends with minimal friction, move comfortably with keyboard or touch, and always have an obvious next activity without losing the freedom to explore.

### Brand Personality
Playful, adventurous, toyetic. The interface should evoke the excitement of opening a premium playset: bold silhouettes, cheerful color, tangible controls, lively characters, and clear feedback without visual chaos.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use an original block-built adventure aesthetic inspired by the supplied references, without copying Zoo or Oof, Roblox branding, characters, logos, or layouts. Favor chunky geometric animal silhouettes, thick ink-like outlines, bright sky-lit worlds, soft low-poly terrain, and a dominant plum/magenta/teal palette: #3B0855, #852467, #FD8083, #EE227D, #498099, and #30C0B7. Use warm cream and deep plum as supporting neutrals. Avoid administrative dashboards, generic gradient-panel grids, emoji-led controls, glassmorphism, cyan-on-black sci-fi styling, and tiny text.

### Platforms and Interaction
Desktop supports mouse, keyboard, and optional gamepad-style controls. Tablet and phone support touch in portrait and landscape, with 44px minimum targets, thumb-reachable movement/action controls, safe-area insets, and no hover-only behavior. Layouts adapt structurally rather than merely shrinking. Motion must remain smooth on mid-range mobile hardware and honor prefers-reduced-motion.

### Design Principles
1. Make the next playful action unmistakable within three seconds.
2. Treat every screen as part of one colorful toy world, not a separate web dashboard.
3. Use bold shape, scale, and animation for hierarchy; keep labels short and highly legible.
4. Preserve full functionality across desktop, tablet, and mobile while adapting control placement to each context.
5. Prefer deterministic project-owned vector and Phaser-generated assets so characters remain stylistically consistent and performant.
6. Use motion to communicate locomotion, terrain, success, and state changes; provide reduced-motion alternatives.
